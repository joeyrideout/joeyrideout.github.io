What I Do

I am interning as an Information Security Analyst at Kinaxis, a supply chain management software supplier for clients ranging from Samsung Electronics to Lockheed Martin Corporation. Part of my role includes maintaining my registered Controlled Goods clearance and upholding our SOC 2 Type II compliance.

I will soon enter my final year as an Honours Computer Science Co-op student at the University of Waterloo in Canada. My hobbies include doing improv, writing, and studying for the ISC² Associate CISSP exam. My other bookmarks are currently wedged in philosophy, history, and public speaking.

Creatively, I am actively writing a body of information security advice for the general public that I plan to publish as videos on The Rideout Hideout. Some rough early videos I made attracted hundreds of thousands of views on Facebook.

My Background: Cybersecurity and Software Engineering

I got started in the technology industry seven years ago when I read a book about cryptography in middle school and later taught myself to code in order to build my own password cracker. After graduating high school in my home province of Alberta, Canada, I moved across the country to join the world's largest co-op system and Canada's leading Computer Science program at UW. I have since completed four internships: three as a full stack Web Developer, and one as a Security Engineer at TINT in San Francisco.
